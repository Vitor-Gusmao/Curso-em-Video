Modern Cordel

By Milton Duarte

I'm getting tired
From such technology
We only speak via email
Short and cold message
Twitter and Facebook
Before I expire
I will say everything in poetry.

It's not like it was before
It's all abbreviated
"You" only has two letters
The "O" and "E" have been crossed out
To declare love
Just put a flower
And a drawn heart.

Arroba now doesn't weigh
It is part of an address
Full stop is not even used
Or go to the beginning
Now it's .com
If the website is very good
He is worth a high price.

To make the language worse
The emoticon is a risk
There's a symbol for everything
Semicolon and an asterisk
A kiss means
To understand how it is
Decipher this scribble.

I miss the letters
Written with your own hand
Sent in June
It only arrived in the summer
But it killed the longing
It was real text
In the lines of the heart.

Now I write and send
Arriving at the same time
But when I'm going to proceed
The person left
Opened another application
The world was captive
From the technology of now.

Fortunately, to pray
No internet needed
God listens to everyone
If you want, take this test
Two points are two knees
Your lips are braces
Let God interpret.
